About

01 / Front Matter

I'm Adenle Joshua, publishing under the name Josh Deck. Over more than 100 titles — my own catalog and client work across fiction and nonfiction — I've built a repeatable system for taking a book from idea to a commercially positioned product on Amazon.

My catalog spans genre fiction (romance and paranormal) and practical nonfiction guides built for KDP's beginner-to-intermediate reader. I've navigated everything a working publisher runs into: category and title-similarity rejections, keyword competition, cover-thumbnail testing, and building a catalog strategy that survives platform changes rather than one lucky launch.

I also run a mentorship program teaching independent authors and aspiring publishers this exact workflow — which means I don't just do the work, I can explain every decision inside it, clearly, to a client who's never touched KDP before.

My Publishing Process

03 / Chapters
01

Validate the idea

Research 2–3 top-performing books in your target category. Identify what separates strong performers from weak ones — pacing, positioning, tag strategy — before a word is written.

02

Build the blueprint

A full concept and structure document: story or content outline, audience positioning, competitive angle, and — for fiction — a beat-by-beat plot map built to platform acceptance criteria.

03

Write to the market, not around it

Chapters written in the pacing and voice that performs in your category — dialogue-forward, hook-driven, reader-tested structure from page one.

04

Optimize for discovery

Keywords, categories, title and subtitle architecture, and a description built to convert — so the book can actually be found.

05

Package and launch

Cover direction, interior formatting, metadata, and a publishing roadmap handed over ready to upload — or fully managed to publish.

Both — depending on what you need. I can run the full process end-to-end (research through launch), or plug in at a single stage: outline, ghostwriting, keyword/metadata optimization, or editorial pass.
Do you work in fiction, nonfiction, or both?
Both. My catalog includes genre fiction (romance, paranormal, drama) and practical nonfiction guides. The research-first approach applies to either.
Can you help if I already have a manuscript?
Yes. I can run a competitive and keyword audit on an existing manuscript, then rebuild positioning, metadata, and cover direction around what's already written.
What platforms do you publish on?
Primarily Amazon KDP, with fiction experience on GoodNovel's contract program and audiobook distribution through FindAway Voices.

I've navigated title-similarity rejections and category strategy directly across a real catalog, so title and metadata decisions are made to avoid these issues upfront, not fixed after a rejection.
